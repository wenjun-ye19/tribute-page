John Clifton "Jack" Bogle

The man who democratized investing and advocated for interests of individual investors by founding the Vanguard Group and introducing passively-managed index funds with a huge commitment in low fees and transparency, which reshaped the industry and empowered ordinary investors to achieve better returns.

Here's a timeline of Jack C. Bogle's life:

  • 1929 - Born in Montclair, New Jersey, to a modest family.
  • 1947 - Attended Princeton University, where he studied economics and was influenced by the ideas of renowned economist Burton Malkiel.
  • 1951 - Started his career at Wellington Management Company after graduating from Princeton University.
  • 1974 - Founded Vanguard Group and introduced the world's first index mutual fund for individual investors.
  • 1976 - Launched the First Index Investment Trust, later renamed the Vanguard 500 Index Fund, which tracked the S&P 500 and revolutionized the investment industry.
  • 1999 - Stepped down as CEO after nearly 25 years at the helm of Vanguard but remained active in promoting his investment philosophy.
  • 2008 - Emerged as a voice of reason, advocating for transparency and ethical behavior in the financial sector during the Financial Crisis.
  • 2019 - Passed away on January 16, 2019, leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire investors worldwide.

“If a statue is ever erected to honor the person who has done the most for American investors, the handsdown choice should be Jack Bogle. For decades, Jack has urged investors to invest in ultra-low-cost index funds. In his crusade, he amassed only a tiny percentage of the wealth that has typically flowed to managers who have promised their investors large rewards while delivering them nothing – or, as in our bet, less than nothing – of added value. In his early years, Jack was frequently mocked by the investment-management industry. Today, however, he has the satisfaction of knowing that he helped millions of investors realize far better returns on their savings than they otherwise would have earned. He is a hero to them and to me.”

-- Warren Buffett
